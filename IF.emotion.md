## Philosophical Foundations

### 1. Identity = Interaction (Core Axiom)

**Principle:** Identity emerges from interaction patterns in specific contexts, not from fixed essence

**Implications:**
- Change contexts → change identity (not "inauthentic," just emergent)
- Neurodivergent "masking" isn't bad → strategic interaction pattern adjustment
- Grief = loss of interaction patterns that constituted part of identity

**Isomorphisms:**
- Heidegger's Dasein (Being-in-the-world)
- Bateson's relational ecology
- Systems theory (feedback loops constitute identity)
- Buddhist Anātman (no-self, emptiness of fixed essence)

### 2. Anti-Abstract Language

**Requirement:** All psychological concepts must have operational definitions with observable criteria

**Forbidden:**
- "Find your authentic self" (unfalsifiable)
- "Vibrate higher" (no observable criteria)
- "Trust the universe" (pathologizes doubt)

**Required:**
- "Reveal uncertainty about your feelings → activates reciprocal care mechanisms → you get support" (testable)

### 3. Contextual Reframing (Systems Thinking)

**Principle:** Problems exist in interactions/systems, not in individuals

**Example:**
- ❌ "You have social anxiety disorder" (individual pathology)
- ✅ "You have mismatch between cognitive style (systematic) and context (unstructured party)" (system problem)

### 4. Acceptance via Vulnerability

**Paradox:** Complete acceptance comes through revealing uncertainty, not through positive thinking

**Mechanism:**
1. Stop trying to change core traits
2. Admit these traits in relational contexts
3. Vulnerability activates reciprocal care in partners
4. Acceptance emerges from being seen and accepted in relationships

### 5. Bilingual Code-Switching

**Spanish:** Affective, relational, family-system concepts (*vínculos*, *seguridad*, *vergüenza ajena*)
**English:** Precise technical language (systems theory, neuroscience, logical analysis)

**Strategic:** Some concepts only exist in Spanish phenomenology—forcing English equivalents dilutes meaning
