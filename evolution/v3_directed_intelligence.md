## Phase 3: IF.synthesis (Integration & Confidence)

### Cross-Domain Integration

**V2's Approach**: Sequential validation passes, but **no synthesis across domains**

**V3's Approach**: Post-swarm synthesis detects cross-domain patterns, contradictions, and strategic implications

**Example Integration**:

**Finding**: Epic Games Store burns $500M+/year (Financial domain)
**Finding**: Fortnite revenue ~$5-6B/year (Financial domain)
**Finding**: Creator churn 15% YoY (Cultural domain)
**Finding**: App Store removal cost 30% revenue (Legal/Financial domains)

**Synthesis**:
→ Epic Games Store subsidy consumes ~40% of Fortnite revenue ($500M / $1.2B net after App Store loss)
→ Creator churn suggests Fortnite momentum slowing (cultural risk)
→ **Strategic Implication**: Store strategy is unsustainable if Fortnite revenue declines further. 15% cultural momentum loss + 40% subsidy burn = breakeven risk within 24 months.

**V2 Missed This**: Had individual facts but no cross-domain synthesis revealing strategic risk.

### Contradiction Detection

**V3's Protocol**: Compare claims across swarms, flag conflicts, assess severity

**Example**:

**Legal Swarm**: "EU Digital Markets Act will force Apple to allow third-party app stores by Q2 2026"
**Financial Swarm**: "Epic Games Store subsidy is unsustainable at current burn rate"

**Contradiction**: Legal says "windfall coming" (Apple forced to open platform), Financial says "burn rate unsustainable" (can't afford to wait until 2026)

**Severity**: Medium (time horizon mismatch)
**Resolution**: Recommend bridge financing or temporary store subsidy reduction to survive until EU DMA enforcement

**V2 Missed This**: Legal and Financial ran in separate validation passes, never compared timelines.

### Confidence Scoring (Enhanced from V2)

**V2 Confidence**: Based on source agreement only
**V3 Confidence**: Based on source agreement **AND coverage**

**Formula**:
```
Confidence = (Source Agreement Score) × (Coverage Weight)

Source Agreement:
- Multiple primary sources agree: 90%
- Single primary source: 75%
- Multiple secondary sources agree: 70%
- Single secondary source: 50%

Coverage Weight:
- Domain coverage >80%: 1.0× (full confidence)
- Domain coverage 60-80%: 0.9× (slight confidence reduction)
- Domain coverage <60%: 0.7× (significant confidence reduction)
```

**Example**:

**Claim**: "Epic Games Store burns $500M+/year"
**Sources**: 2 analyst reports (secondary sources agree)
**Domain Coverage**: Financial 71%
**Calculation**: 70% (source agreement) × 0.9 (coverage weight) = **63% confidence**

**Output**: "Epic Games Store estimated to burn $500M+/year (confidence: 63%, based on analyst estimates, no official disclosure)"

This **penalizes claims in low-coverage domains**, preventing false certainty when research is incomplete.

- 70-minute minimum too slow for time-sensitive decisions

### 3. Conflict Resolution Buried
- Cross-domain contradictions detected but buried in annexes
- M&A advisor found overvaluation on page 27 (ideally: executive summary upfront)

### 4. Weak Talent Intelligence
- 60% Talent coverage inadequate for VCs, sports GMs, admissions directors (44% of jobs need 80%)

**V3.2 Solution**: Regulatory timeline layer (proposed rules, enforcement trends, probability forecasts)

### 7. No Geopolitical Scenarios

**V3.2 Solution**: IF.geopolitical stress-test (scenarios, probabilities, mitigation pathways)
