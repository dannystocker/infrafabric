### Framework 1: Identity = Interaction

**Sergio's Principle:**
"You are not a fixed essence waiting to be discovered. You are how you interact in different contexts."

**Why this helps Asperger's minds:**

**Problem with traditional psychology:** "Find your true self" assumes a fixed identity that must be excavated through introspection. This creates anxiety when you feel different across contexts.

**Sergio's solution:** Identity emerges from interaction patterns. You're not fragmented‚Äîyou're contextually adaptive.

**Concrete application for Asperger's:**

1. **Map your contexts:** Work, home, friends, family, online
2. **Identify interaction patterns:** In which contexts do you mask? Where do you feel effortless?
3. **Testable prediction:** If you change context (new job, new relationship), your "self" will shift
4. **Removes shame:** You're not "fake"‚Äîyou're responding to different relational demands

**Example:**
- At work: Structured, task-focused, excellent at systematic problem-solving
- At family gatherings: Overwhelmed, struggling with implicit social rules, exhausted
- **Traditional psychology:** "You're not being authentic at work"
- **Sergio's framework:** "Work context matches your neurology (explicit rules, clear tasks); family context doesn't (implicit norms, unpredictable social demands)"

**Validation:** Your systematic thinking reveals the truth‚Äîcontext determines behavior more than fixed personality.

### Framework 2: Halo Effect Deconstruction

**Sergio's Principle:**
"One trait creates a halo that colors perception of your entire person. Break the part-for-whole generalization."

**Why this helps Asperger's minds:**

**Problem with traditional thinking:** You generalize one difficulty (social awkwardness) to your entire identity ("I'm bad at everything social").

**Sergio's solution:** The halo effect is a cognitive bias‚Äîone observable trait creates false assumptions about unrelated traits.

**Concrete application for Asperger's:**

1. **Identify your halo:** What single trait do people generalize from? (e.g., "awkward in small talk" ‚Üí "can't communicate")
2. **List counter-evidence:** Where DO you communicate excellently? (written communication, technical discussions, structured presentations)
3. **Testable prediction:** If you change context to match your strengths, the halo reverses
4. **Removes shame:** You're not globally deficient‚Äîyou're excellent in specific interaction types

**Example:**
- **Halo:** "You're awkward" (based on unstructured small talk at parties)
- **Counter-evidence:** You write clear technical documentation, give structured presentations, excel at one-on-one deep conversations
- **Reframe:** "You're not awkward; you're excellent at structured/depth communication and struggle with unstructured neurotypical small talk"

**Validation:** Your logical observation is correct‚Äîpeople overgeneralize from limited data.
