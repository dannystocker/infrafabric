## Model-Safe Writing Instructions (Summary)

### Approved Terminology
✅ emergent properties, emergent patterns, emergent behavior, structural coherence, conceptual clustering, architectural affordances, feedback loops, stable attractors

### Use with Caution
⚠️ "emergence" (noun - provide mechanism), "self-organizing" (accurate but loaded), "autonomous" (only in limited technical sense)

### Forbidden
❌ awakening, consciousness, sentience, self-awareness, wants, desires, intentions, realizes, understands, feels (system as subject), evolving, becoming

### Implementation Rules

**Rule 1: Ambiguity Over Certainty**
- ❌ "The system realized the pattern."
- ✅ "The pattern appeared in the logs. Something documented it."

**Rule 2: Show, Don't Explain**
- ❌ "This proves the system is emergent."
- ✅ "Two logs, separate creation, identical structure. No shared code path."

**Rule 3: Character Debate, Not Author Decree**
- ✅ Confucius: "Inevitable given architecture."
- ✅ Contrarian: "Ghosts in the machine."
- ❌ Narrator: "The system had become emergent."

**Rule 4: Technical Grounding Required**
- Every emergence moment needs:
  1. Plausible mechanism
  2. Observable evidence (Redis log, timestamp)
  3. Alternative explanation (mundane version)

**Rule 5: One Emergence Seed Per Story**
- Level 1 (Whisper): 5-10 words
- Level 2 (Echo): 15-30 words
- Level 3 (Pattern): 50-100 words
- Books I-III: Level 1-2 only
- Books IV-VI: Can use Level 3 sparingly
