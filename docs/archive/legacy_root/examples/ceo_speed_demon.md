## Lessons Learned

### 1. Speed Enables Better Decisions (Counterintuitive)
**Assumption**: Faster analysis = lower quality decision
**Reality**: Faster analysis = **more prep time** = higher quality presentation = better board alignment

**CEO had 95 minutes to prepare** (V3.2) vs. 40 minutes (V3):
- Built confident recommendation (not rushed speculation)
- Anticipated board questions (CFO asked about confidence, CEO was ready)
- Practiced presentation (delivered calmly, not frantically)

**Lesson**: **Speed doesn't sacrifice quality if coverage is targeted**. 68% coverage on relevant domains beats 82% coverage if you're too rushed to use it.

---

### 2. Not All Domains Matter for All Decisions
**V3 Philosophy**: Comprehensive coverage across all domains (Legal, Financial, Technical, Cultural, Talent)
**V3.2 Insight**: **Domain relevance varies by decision type**

**For pricing decisions**:
- ✅ Financial (critical): Competitor's pricing strategy, margin implications
- ✅ Technical (critical): Product differentiation, switching costs
- ✅ Legal (important): Regulatory constraints on pricing
- ❌ Cultural (nice-to-have): Customer sentiment (matters for long-term, not immediate response)
- ❌ Talent (irrelevant): Competitor's executive team (doesn't affect pricing strategy)

**Lesson**: **Audience presets should skip irrelevant domains**, not research everything equally. This is V3.2's core innovation.
