This created a credibility paradox: **If Angelique truly co-created this with professional supply chain expertise, that's a powerful endorsement. If that fact is hidden, it's a missed persuasion opportunity.**

Two possibilities:
1. **Angelique was a prior conversation partner, not a named resource.** If so, keep her anonymous.

## The Timing Discrepancy

A small but important finding: The document says **Protocole 15:30** (Mediafret status push), but the user said **15:50 deadline** for status confirmation.

20-minute difference. Might be nothing. Might indicate:
- Document is outdated
- User has more recent information
- Different interpretation of what "deadline" means

But in operational documents, 20 minutes is material. A 15:50 deadline creates a 15-minute decision buffer before alerting chantiers at 16:00. A 15:30 push creates a 30-minute buffer. Different risk profiles.

This reinforced the finding: **The operational context needs explicit verification with Adrien/team before presentation.**

## What I'd Do Differently

**1. Verify Operational Context Earlier**
I should have flagged the Angelique/buyer/GESI/timing gaps as blockers earlier, not discovered them in the operational verification step. A 5-minute clarifying question at session start ("Tell me about Angelique and the buyer dynamics") would have prevented late discovery.

**2. Separate Document vs. Framework Discussion**
By end of session, we were discussing both the specific GEDIMAT proposal AND the universal evaluation framework. These are related but different conversations. Would have been clearer to say: "Document is 88.75/100 and ready. Framework for all future variants is v1.0 and reusable. Here are the cleanup tasks for each."

**3. Address Political Dynamics Explicitly**
The mention of "new buyer wants to change Angelique's role; Angelique disagrees" is organizational tension. The document should have a section addressing "how the new buyer applies their preferred processes within this framework." Didn't address that gap clearly.
