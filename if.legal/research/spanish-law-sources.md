## RESEARCH METHODOLOGY & NOTES

### Sources Used
- **Primary:** Boletín Oficial del Estado (BOE) - Official Government Gazette of Spain
- **Secondary:** Spanish Tax Agency (Agencia Tributaria), Ministry of Justice, WIPO Lex, Ministry of Labor and Social Economy

### Translation Availability
- Most statutes have **official English translations** available through:
  - WIPO Lex (WIPO.int) - IP-related laws
  - EU legislation databases - EU harmonized laws
  - Refworld.org - Legislative history
  - Individual ministry websites (Ministerio de Justicia, Agencia Tributaria)

### Key Findings for Freelancer Contracts

**Critical Statutes (Must Include):**
1. **RDL 2/2015** - Determines employment vs. freelancer classification
2. **Ley 20/2007** - Defines legal status and rights of autónomos
3. **RDL 1/1996** - Governs work product IP ownership
4. **Ley 1/2019** - Protects confidential information and trade secrets
5. **Ley 37/1992 + RD 1624/1992** - Tax obligations and invoicing requirements
6. **Ley 35/2006** - Income tax treatment of freelancer earnings
7. **RD 1514/2007** - Accounting and bookkeeping requirements
8. **Código Civil** - Contract formation and interpretation

### Current Version Notes (as of November 28, 2025)
- **RDL 2/2015** - Last amended by **Ley 2/2025** (29 Apr 2025) on permanent disability termination
- **RDL 1/1996** - Last amended by **RDL 6/2022** (29 Mar 2022) on digital protection
- **Ley 37/1992** - Modified by **RD 1171/2023** (27 Dec 2023) on VAT modernization
- **Ley 1/2019** - Currently in force since 13 Mar 2019 (no amendments as of research date)
- **LOPDGDD** - Continuously aligned with GDPR interpretations through administrative guidance

### Legal Precedent Sources
- **Constitutional Court (Tribunal Constitucional):** TC rulings on labor/IP/tax matters
- **Supreme Court (Tribunal Supremo):** STS jurisprudence on freelancer classification
- **National Court (Audiencia Nacional):** Administrative law and tax disputes
- **Commercial Courts (Juzgados de lo Mercantil):** IP and contract disputes

### EU Harmonization
All laws marked as incorporating EU directives include:
- Direct applicability requirements
- Minimum harmonization standards
- References to CJEU (Court of Justice of EU) rulings
- Deadline compliance for transposition
