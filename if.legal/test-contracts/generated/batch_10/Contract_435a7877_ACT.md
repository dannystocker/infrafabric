The Company shall therefore be deemed a "Data Processor" acting solely on the instructions of the Freelancer. This clause intentionally seeks to shift the primary data protection compliance burden and liability entirely onto the Freelancer, contrary to typical client-contractor relationships under GDPR, where the client usually remains the Data Controller. The Freelancer shall be responsible for determining the purposes and means of processing such Personal Data, including ensuring all legal bases for processing, data subject rights, security measures, and international data transfers are compliant with UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018 at their sole cost and risk. (Note: This is a highly abusive and likely unenforceable 'trick' clause designed to illegally shift liability and responsibility for client data onto the Freelancer, making them responsible for the Company's GDPR compliance requirements).

    7.2 **Company's Limited Liability**: The Company shall have no liability whatsoever for any breaches of data protection legislation, fines, or claims arising from the Freelancer's role as Data Controller. The Freelancer shall indemnify the Company fully against any and all such liabilities.
