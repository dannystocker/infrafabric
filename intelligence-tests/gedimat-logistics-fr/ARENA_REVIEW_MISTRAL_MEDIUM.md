## 1. IF.TTT Compliance: PASS
**Score:** 98/100
**Unsourced Gedimat claims found:** 0

**Verdict:** EXEMPLARY. The dossier rigorously applies IF.TTT (Traceable, Transparent, Trustworthy) methodology with exceptional discipline. Every financial or operational claim about Gedimat is presented in one of three acceptable formats:

1. **Formula with placeholders:** `RSI = [Baseline factures 30j] / [Investissement] × [8-15%]`
2. **Explicit hypothesis label:** "Application hypothétique Gedimat (À VALIDER avec données réelles)"
3. **Observable process metric:** "% of orders assigned by proximity score"

This approach completely eliminates "phantom numbers"—the consulting industry's chronic credibility problem where impressive-sounding projections ("€2M annual savings!") are based on opaque assumptions or unverifiable industry averages.

**Why this matters strategically:** The formula-first approach creates a *collaborative* dynamic with the CFO ("let's plug in our real numbers and validate the model together") rather than a *confrontational* one ("why should I believe your savings claim?"). This is a genuine methodological innovation that should become industry standard.

**Minor deduction rationale:** While execution is near-perfect, 1-2 sections could benefit from even more explicit data requirement statements. For example, the "Scoring de Proximité V1" could include: "Pré-requis data : Export ERP des adresses chantier (format CSV minimum : code postal, ville, coordonnées GPS si disponibles)."

- **Tech startups:** Anglicisms widely accepted

- **C-suite vs. operational teams:** Executives generally prefer formal French terminology

Given that Gedimat operates in négoce (building materials distribution)—a traditional French industry—conservative language choices are strategically safer for board presentation.
