## Strengths (What Works Well)

1.  **IF.TTT Methodology:** Its greatest strength. By refusing to present unsourced numbers, the dossier builds immense credibility and invites collaboration (i.e., "let's plug in *our* numbers") rather than skepticism. This is a powerful and innovative approach.
2.  **Behavioral Science Integration:** The framing of logistics as a tool for client loyalty, not just cost reduction, is sophisticated and strategic. It elevates the conversation and aligns operational improvements with higher-level business goals.
3.  **Pragmatic Actionability:** The focus on "Quick Wins" using existing resources makes the proposal feel low-risk and immediately valuable. It avoids the common consulting pitfall of recommending expensive, long-term studies or software purchases as a first step.
4.  **C-Suite Calibrated Communication:** The "Résumé Exécutif" is a masterclass in concise, high-impact communication. The language, tone, and structure are perfectly tailored for a board-level audience.

---

## Weaknesses (What Needs Improvement)

1.  **Potential Underestimation of Change Management:** While the quick wins are operationally simple, the dossier could briefly mention the human element of change. Ensuring Angélique feels empowered and supported, and that drivers understand the new processes, will be key. This is a minor point, as the "Gouvernance Comportementale" section touches upon this.
2.  **Scalability Challenges:** The initial solution is rightly focused on Excel and manual processes. The dossier could benefit from a single sentence in the "Moyen Terme" section acknowledging that if the pilot is successful, a more scalable, automated solution (e.g., a lightweight TMS) would be the logical next step to institutionalize the gains.
