## 5. French Language Quality: CONDITIONAL
**Score:** 88/100
**Anglicisms Section 1:** 6
**Anglicisms total:** 10+ (including "Quick Win/Quick Wins", "ROI", "dashboard", "KPI", "benchmark")
**Tone:** Professional and appropriate for C-suite audience

**Verdict:** The French is clear, concise, and business-appropriate. However, the recurring anglicisms are the document's most significant and easily fixable weakness. For a French C-suite document in 2025, especially in a traditional industry like building materials (négoce), conservative language choices are strategically safer.

**Cultural consideration:** In my assessment, the use of anglicisms in French business documents falls on a spectrum:
- **Tech startups:** Anglicisms widely accepted ("ROI", "KPI", "dashboard")
- **Traditional industries (construction, manufacturing):** More conservative; excessive anglicisms can be perceived as unprofessional or culturally tone-deaf
- **C-suite vs. operational teams:** Executives often prefer formal French business terminology

Given that Gedimat operates in a traditional French industry, I recommend eliminating all anglicisms:

**Required replacements:**
- "Quick Win" / "Quick Wins" → "Gain Rapide" / "Gains Rapides"
- "ROI" → "RSI" (Retour sur Investissement - standard French business term)
- "dashboard" → "tableau de bord"
- "KPI" → "ICP" (Indicateur Clé de Performance)
- "benchmark" → "référence externe" or "point de comparaison sectoriel"

**Estimated fix time:** 20-30 minutes with find-and-replace plus manual review.
