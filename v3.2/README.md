## Philosophy Simplification (90% Guidance Complexity Reduction)

### The Problem
Original IF.philosophy database references 12 philosophers across 2,400 years:
- **Western**: Locke (1689), Peirce (1877), Vienna Circle (1920s), Duhem (1906), Quine (1951), James (1907), Dewey (1938), Popper (1934), Epictetus (125 CE)
- **Eastern**: Buddha (500 BCE), Lao Tzu (6th century BCE), Confucius (551-479 BCE)

Users don't need philosophy lessonsâ€”they need practical principles.

### The Solution (V3.2)

**Six Practical Principles** (user-facing):

1. **Show Me the Evidence** (Locke, Vienna Circle)
   *Every claim traces to observable artifact*

2. **We Might Be Wrong** (Peirce)
   *Explicit confidence scores + known gaps*

3. **Do the Pieces Fit?** (Quine)
   *Contradiction detection across sources*

4. **How Could We Be Wrong?** (Popper)
   *Falsification tests before committing*

5. **What Works in Practice?** (James, Dewey)
   *Utility over perfect truth*

6. **Admit What You Don't Know** (Buddha, Lao Tzu)
   *Transparent uncertainty metrics*

**Impact**: Users understand guidance in 2 minutes instead of 20 minutes. Philosophical rigor preserved in underlying components.
