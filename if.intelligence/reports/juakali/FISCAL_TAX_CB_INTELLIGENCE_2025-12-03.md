## TIER 1: KENYA, NIGERIA, GHANA

### KENYA

#### Tax Regime
- **Corporate Tax Rate:** 25% (standard rate for companies)
- **Withholding Tax (WHT) on Interest:** 15%
- **Withholding Tax (WHT) on Dividends:** 8% for resident shareholders
- **Withholding Tax on Asset Realization:** 3% (resident), 10% (non-resident)
- **VAT on Financial Services:** 16% standard rate (some financial services exempt)
- **Branch Repatriation Tax:** 15% effective January 2024 for foreign branches repatriating profits
- **Digital Services Tax:** SEP tax at effective rate of 3% (replaced 1.5% DST in 2024)
- **Source:** [PWC Kenya Tax Summaries](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/kenya/corporate/tax-administration), [Kenya Finance Bill 2024](https://www.ey.com/en_gl/technical/tax-alerts/kenya-proposes-tax-changes-under-the-finance-bill--2024)

#### Central Bank Circulars & Regulations
- **Circular/Law:** Business Laws (Amendment) Act, 2024 (effective December 2024)
  - **Key Requirement:** Reclassifies "Digital Lenders" as "Non-Deposit Taking Credit Providers (NDTCs)" alongside traditional MFIs under unified CBK regulation
  - **Compliance Deadline:** All NDTCs must apply for CBK license by June 27, 2025
  - **Status:** As of October 2024, 85 out of 730+ DCP applications licensed
  - **Source:** [CR Advocates Kenya](https://www.cradvocatesllp.com/legal-alert-recent-amendments-in-kenyan-business-laws-impacting-digital-credit-providers-and-microfinance-institutions/)

- **Minimum Capital Requirements:**
  - **Deposit-Taking MFIs:** KES 1 billion minimum core capital (progressive increase to KES 10 billion by December 2029)
  - **Community-level DTMs:** KES 20 million
  - **Nationwide DTMs:** KES 60 million
  - **Source:** [CBK Licensing Procedures](https://www.centralbank.go.ke/images/docs/Licensing%20Procedures/LICENSINGMICROFINANCEBANK.pdf)

- **Interest Rate Caps:** NO current interest rate cap for MFIs/digital lenders (2016 cap repealed in 2019)
  - **CBK Governor Statement (2024):** "We are not going into that part of the world again" - Patrick Njoroge confirmed no plans to cap DCP interest rates
  - **Source:** [Standard Media Kenya](https://www.standardmedia.co.ke/business/news/article/2001430611/cbk-will-not-cap-interest-rates-charged-by-digital-lenders)

- **AML/KYC Requirements:**
  - **Legislation:** Proceeds of Crime and Anti-Money Laundering Act (POCAMLA, 2009)
  - **Requirement:** All MFIs and NDTCs must register with Financial Reporting Centre
  - **Enhanced Due Diligence:** Required for cross-border transactions, reflecting FATF recommendations
  - **Data Protection:** Must comply with Section 19 of Data Protection Act, 2019
  - **Source:** [Fintech Laws Kenya 2025](https://iclg.com/practice-areas/fintech-laws-and-regulations/kenya)

- **Data Localization:** No explicit data localization requirement for financial institutions (as of 2024)

#### Minister/Governor Statements
- **Date:** February 2025
  **Speaker:** Cabinet Secretary John Mbadi (National Treasury)
  **Quote:** "The National Financial Inclusion Strategy 2025-2028 is a call to action to create a vibrant, efficient, and inclusive financial system that benefits all Kenyans."
  **Context:** Launch of NFIS 2025-2028, targeting to double financially healthy adults to 40% by 2028 (from 18.3% currently)
  **Source:** [Kenya NFIS 2025-2028](https://vellum.co.ke/unpacking-the-draft-kenya-national-financial-inclusion-strategy-nfis-2025-2028/)

- **Date:** November 2024
  **Initiative:** "Hustler Fund" (Financial Inclusion Fund)
  **Amount:** KES 60 billion disbursed to 24.6 million customers
  **Repeat Customers:** 8.5 million beneficiaries
  **Repayment Rate:** 79%
  **Source:** [Kenya Budget Policy Statement 2024-2025](http://www.parliament.go.ke/sites/default/files/2024-03/Report%20of%20the%20Budget%20Policy%20Statement%20for%20FY%202024-25%20and%20the%20Medium%20Term_0.pdf)

#### Licensing Requirements
- **Application Process:** Submit to CBK Governor indicating MFI category, accompanied by memorandum/articles, registered business location, and capital evidence
- **Timeline:** Minimum 6 months for Application in Principle (AIP) processing from complete application
- **Capital Requirements:** See above (KES 20M to KES 10B depending on tier and timeline)
- **Approval Requirements:** Promoters and management must pass "fit and proper" assessment; borrowed money cannot be used as capital
- **Source:** [CBK Microfinance Regulations](https://www.centralbank.go.ke/policy-procedures/legislation-and-guidelines/microfinance-_deposit-taking-microfinance-institutions_-regulations/)

### NIGERIA

#### Tax Regime
- **Corporate Tax Rate:** 30% (standard rate for companies including financial institutions)
- **Withholding Tax (WHT) on Interest:** 10% (standard)
- **Withholding Tax (WHT) on Dividends:** 10% (standard)
- **Withholding Tax (WHT) on Royalties:** 10%
- **Withholding Tax on Management/Professional Fees:** 10%
- **VAT on Financial Services:**
  - **Unit MFBs (Tier 1 & 2):** EXEMPT from VAT per Value Added Tax (Modification) Order, 2021
  - **State & National MFBs:** Subject to VAT (exemption limited to Unit MFBs only)
  - **Other Financial Institutions:** 7.5% VAT applies to commissions, fees, and charges (interest on loans/advances NOT subject to VAT)
- **Windfall Tax on Banks:** Introduced in 2024 Budget (contentious)
- **Source:** [FIRS VAT Circular on Financial Institutions](https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/tax-authorities/1075220/firs-issues-information-circular-on-the-chargeability-of-vat-on-services-of-financial-institutions), [Nigeria Corporate Tax PWC](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/Nigeria/Corporate/Significant-developments)

#### Central Bank Circulars & Regulations
- **Minimum Capital Requirements (Effective 2024):**
  - **Tier 1 Unit MFB:** NGN 200 million (operate in urban areas, max 5 branches within 5 contiguous LGAs)
  - **Tier 2 Unit MFB:** NGN 50 million (rural/underbanked areas, 1 branch office within same LGA)
  - **State MFB:** NGN 1 billion (statewide operations, max 2 branches per LGA initially)
  - **National MFB:** NGN 5 billion (nationwide operations, multiple states)
  - **Source:** [Lexology Nigeria MFB Licensing](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=b149e218-5064-45ca-9a9f-1a3e72cc4449), [ICA Nigeria MFB Guide 2025](https://ica.ng/how-to-get-a-microfinance-bank-license-in-nigeria-a-simple-step‑by‑step-guide/)

- **Commercial Banks Recapitalization (April 2024):**
  - **National Commercial Banks:** NGN 200 billion minimum
  - **International Banks:** NGN 500 billion minimum
  - **Note:** This primarily affects commercial banks, not MFBs
  - **Source:** [Templars Law Nigeria](https://www.templars-law.com/app/uploads/2024/04/Client-Alert-CBN-New-Minimum-Capital-Requirements-for-Banks.pdf)

- **Application Fees:**
  - **Unit MFB:** NGN 50,000 (non-refundable)
  - **State MFB:** NGN 100,000 (non-refundable)
  - **National MFB:** NGN 250,000 (non-refundable)
  - **Licensing Fee:** NGN 250K / NGN 500K / NGN 1M respectively
  - **Stamp Duty:** 0.75% of capital
  - **Timeline:** AIP processing 90+ days; final license 3-6 months post-AIP = 6-9 months end-to-end
  - **Source:** [Chaman Law Firm Nigeria](https://chamanlawfirm.com/the-requirements-for-obtaining-micro-finance-bank-licence-in-nigeria/)

- **Data Localization & Cloud Computing:**
  - **Regulation:** CBN IT Standards Blueprint (Revised 2019), CBN Risk-Based Cybersecurity Framework 2024
  - **Requirement:** Domestic transactions must route through local switch (June 2020 guidelines)
  - **NITDA Requirement (2019):** Sensitive government/citizen data, confidential data, and national security information MUST be stored in Nigeria cloud framework
  - **Sovereign Data:** All subscriber and consumer data must be hosted in Nigeria; transfer outside requires NITDA express approval
  - **Cloud Services:** Permitted for financial institutions if compliance with legal/regulatory requirements maintained; institution remains responsible for function
  - **Source:** [CBN Cybersecurity Framework 2024](https://cbn.gov.ng/Out/2024/BSD/CBN%20Risk-Based%20Cybersecurity%20Framework%20for%20DMBs%20and%20PSBs_2024.pdf), [DPA Digital Digest Nigeria 2025](https://digitalpolicyalert.org/digest/dpa-digital-digest-nigeria)

- **AML/KYC Requirements:**
  - **Legislation:** Money Laundering (Prevention and Prohibition) Act, 2022
  - **Regulator:** Financial Reporting Council of Nigeria (FRCN)
  - **Requirements:** MFBs must implement KYC, customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, and suspicious transaction reporting
  - **Source:** [Fintech 2025 Nigeria](https://practiceguides.chambers.com/practice-guides/fintech-2025/nigeria)

#### Minister/Governor Statements
- **Date:** November 12, 2024
  **Speaker:** Olayemi Cardoso, CBN Governor
  **Event:** 2nd International Financial Inclusion Conference 2024 (IFIC'24)
  **Quote:** "Financial inclusion has the potential to unlock significant economic growth, particularly through the empowerment of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), women and other vulnerable segments of the population."
  **Key Points:**
  - SMEs contribute over 80% of employment in Nigeria but face credit access challenges
  - CBN strengthening Primary Mortgage Banks (PMBs) and Microfinance Banks (MFBs)
  - Integration of PMBs/MFBs into GSI platform to minimize NPLs
  - **Source:** [CBN Financial Inclusion 2024](https://www.cbn.gov.ng/FeaturedArticles/2024/IFIC2024.html)

- **Date:** November 29, 2024
  **Speaker:** Olayemi Cardoso, CBN Governor
  **Event:** Chartered Institute of Bankers of Nigeria (CIBN) Annual Dinner
  **Announcement:** CBN 2026 Strategic Plan prioritizes national payments system upgrade and financial inclusion improvement
  **Initiatives:** Targeted agent banking, microfinance institutions, remittance channels, and mobile money operators
  **Accomplishments:** Clearance of over $7 billion in verified FX backlogs, introduction of BMatch system, Nigeria Payments System Vision 2028
  **Gender Focus:** Launch of CBN Women Financial Inclusion Data Dashboard for centralized reporting on women's market segment
  **Source:** [Proshare Nigeria CBN Governor Speech](https://proshare.co/articles/keynote-address-by-the-governor-of-the-central-bank-of-nigeria-olayemi-cardoso-delivered-at-the-cibn-annual-dinner-november-29-2024?menu=Economy&classification=Read&category=Monetary+Policy)

- **Date:** 2025
  **Speaker:** Aliyu Abubakar, Acting Divisional Head of Other Financial Institutions, CBN Kano
  **Quote:** "Those who cannot obtain services from commercial banks have only one alternative, the microfinance banks."
  **Context:** Emphasizing MFIs' critical role as alternative to commercial banking
  **Source:** [TV360 Nigeria](https://www.tv360nigeria.com/cbn-implements-reforms-to-strengthen-financial-system-and-boost-inclusion-governor-cardoso/)

#### Licensing Requirements
- **Eligibility:** Body corporate incorporated in Nigeria (CAC registration required)
- **Application:** Submit to CBN Governor with memorandum/articles, business location, capital evidence, promoter/management CVs
- **Capital Source:** Must be from bona fide investor funds (NO borrowed money permitted)
- **Timeline:** 6-9 months end-to-end
- **Key Considerations:** Promoters and management must be "approved persons" per regulatory standards; shareholding structure must be disclosed
- **Source:** [Pavestones Legal Nigeria](https://pavestoneslegal.com/licensing-requirements-for-banks-and-other-financial-institutions-in-nigeria/)

### GHANA

#### Tax Regime
- **Corporate Tax Rate:** 25% (standard rate for companies)
- **Financial Sector Recovery Levy (FSRL):** 5% levy on profit before tax of banks (excluding rural/community banks)
  - **Effective Period:** March 2021 - December 2024 (EXPIRED as of December 2024)
  - **Payment:** Quarterly (31 March, 30 June, 30 September, 31 December)
  - **Deductibility:** NOT an allowable deduction in calculating chargeable income
  - **Source:** [Ghana Tax Rates & Dates](https://www.taxlawgh.com/ghana-tax-rates)

- **Growth and Sustainability Levy (GSL):** 2.5% or 5% of profit before tax
  - **Applicable Period:** 2023-2028 tax years
  - **Rate Variation:** 2.5%, 5% of PBT, or 1% of gross production depending on sector
  - **Source:** [Understanding Business Taxes Ghana 2024](https://firmusadvisory.com/2023/05/02/understanding-domestic-business-taxes-in-ghana-a-comprehensive-tax-guide-2023/)

- **Withholding Tax (WHT):**
  - **Dividends (within Ghana):** 8%
  - **Asset Realization:** 3% (resident), 10% (non-resident)
  - **Filing Deadline:** 15th day of subsequent month
  - **Source:** [Ghana Corporate Tax PWC](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/ghana/corporate/withholding-taxes)

- **VAT on Financial Services:** Exempt for microfinance (see note below)
- **Minimum Tax:** 2.2% or 5.5% based on turnover and tax regime

#### Central Bank Circulars & Regulations
- **Minimum Capital Requirements (Tiers):**
  - **Tier 2 - Deposit-Taking Microfinance Companies (MFCs):** GHS 2.0 million
  - **Tier 3 - Micro-Credit Companies:** GHS 2.0 million
  - **Tier 3 - FNGOs (Financial NGOs):** GHS 0.3 million
  - **Tier 4 - Micro-Credit Institutions:** No minimum capital requirement
  - **Digital Lenders (2024):** GHS 2 million with transaction limit capped at GHS 10,000
  - **Supervisor:** Tiers 2 & 3 supervised by OFISD (Other Financial Institutions Supervision Department); Tier 4 supervised by industry associations (MCAG, GCSCA) on behalf of BoG
  - **Source:** [Bank of Ghana Microfinance Requirements](https://www.bog.gov.gh/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/REQUIREMENT-FOR-MFI-LICENCE.pdf), [Ghana Microfinance Analysis](https://cdcconsult.com/ghanas-microfinance-sector-structure-status-capitalisation-and-way-forward/)

- **Shareholding Restrictions:**
  - **Tier 2 (Deposit-Taking MFIs):** Restricted to Ghanaian nationals ONLY
  - **Tier 3 (Non-Deposit-Taking MFIs):** May be exclusively Ghanaian, exclusively foreign, or joint Ghanaian-foreign; foreign capital must be registered with Ghana Investment Promotion Centre
  - **Source:** [BoG Licensing Requirements for MFIs](https://dfsobservatory.com/sites/default/files/Bank%20of%20Ghana%20-%20Licensing%20Requirements%20for%20Microfinance%20Institutions.pdf)

- **Recent Circulars (2024):**
  - **July 2024:** Fit and Proper Standards notice for shareholders, directors, and key management personnel of banks and SDIs
  - **November 2024:** Climate-Related Financial Risk Directive, 2024 for all RFIs
  - **December 2024:** Draft Open Banking Directive released to enhance digital financial services
  - **Source:** [Banking Laws Ghana 2025](https://www.globallegalinsights.com/practice-areas/banking-and-finance-laws-and-regulations/ghana/)

- **AML/KYC Requirements:**
  - **Legislation:** Anti-Money Laundering Act, 2020 (Act 1044); Anti-Terrorism Act, 2008 (Act 762)
  - **Regulator:** Financial Intelligence Centre (FIC)
  - **Requirements:** Customer risk profiling (low/medium/high), transaction monitoring, record-keeping (6 years minimum), sanctions & PEP screening
  - **Penalties:** Up to GHS 1 million fines for inadequate KYC; license revocation by BoG; criminal liability for compliance officers
  - **Example (2024):** Ghanaian fintech fined GHS 500,000 for inadequate PEP screening
  - **FATF Status:** Ghana removed from grey list in June 2021; remains compliant
  - **Source:** [KYC Compliance Ghana 2025](https://blog.voveid.com/kyc-compliance-in-ghana-2025-guide-for-fintechs-and-regulated-businesses/)

#### Minister/Governor Statements
- **Date:** March 2025
  **Speaker:** Dr. Cassiel Ato Forson, Minister for Finance
  **Initiative:** Abolition of Electronic Levy (E-levy) to boost mobile money usage and financial inclusion
  - **Previous Rate:** 1.75% on all mobile money transactions (introduced 2022)
  - **Expected Impact:** Promote financial inclusion, enhance business operations for mobile money providers
  - **Source:** [Ghana 2025 Budget Full Text](https://citinewsroom.com/2025/03/full-text-2025-budget-presented-by-finance-minister/)

- **Key Budget Allocations (2025):**
  - **SME GO Programme:** GHS 8.2 billion mobilized through DBG, GhanaExim Bank, GEA for SMEs
  - **Big Push Programme:** GHS 13.85 billion allocated
  - **District Assembly Common Fund:** GHS 7.51 billion (minimum 80% direct to districts)
  - **Mobile Money Regulation Achievement:** Ghana ranked best in the world for Mobile Money regulation (2024 GSMA Index)
  - **Source:** [Ghana Finance Minister 2025 Allocations](https://www.modernghana.com/news/1385234/2025-finance-minister-outlines-financial-allocati.html)

#### Licensing Requirements
- **Eligibility:** Body corporate incorporated in Ghana; Tier 2 limited to Ghanaians
- **Application Process:** Submit to Bank of Ghana with business plan, capital evidence, governance structure
- **Approval Timeline:** Approximately 6 months (if not granted within 6 months, deemed refused)
- **Capital Evidence:** Must demonstrate minimum capital in corporate accounts
- **Source:** [Processes for MFI Licensing Ghana](https://www.mondaq.com/shareholders/954916/the-processes-for-applying-and-securing-a-microfinance-operating-licence-in-ghana)

## TIER 2: SENEGAL, CÔTE D'IVOIRE, CAMEROON (FRANCOPHONE)

### SENEGAL (WAEMU/BCEAO)

#### Tax Regime
- **Corporate Tax Rate:** Progressive rate structure; minimum CIT of 0.5% on annual turnover (max XOF 5 million)
- **Tax Year:** Calendar year
- **Filing Deadline:** April 30 of following year (CIT returns, simplified transfer pricing return, financial statements)
- **Payment Schedule:** Two instalments (1/3 of previous year's tax each) by February 15 and April 30; balance by June 15
- **Special Tax on Financial Activities (instead of VAT):** 17% on banking, money transfers, interest, premiums, financial intermediation, change operations
- **VAT (General):** 18% standard rate
- **VAT on Digital Services (2024):** Non-resident providers must register from July 1, 2024; must appoint fiscal representative
- **Source:** [Senegal Corporate Tax PWC](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/senegal/corporate/taxes-on-corporate-income), [Senegal VAT Digital Services 2024](https://kpmg.com/us/en/taxnewsflash/news/2024/06/tnf-senegal-changes-vat-cross-border-digital-services.html)

#### Central Bank Circulars & Regulations (BCEAO)
- **Regulatory Authority:** Central Bank of West African States (BCEAO) for WAEMU region
- **Key Regulation:** Instruction No. 001-01-2024 (issued January 23, 2024)
  - **Effective Date:** September 1, 2025 (final deadline after multiple extensions from July 31, 2024 → Jan 31, 2025 → May 1, 2025)
  - **Scope:** Payment services licensing within WAEMU (8 member countries)

- **Minimum Capital Requirements:**
  - **Range:** XOF 10 million to XOF 100 million (approximately $17,400 to $174,000)
  - **Banks (WAEMU-wide):** XOF 20 billion minimum share capital (effective January 1, 2024, increased from XOF 10 billion)
  - **Headquarters Requirement:** Fintechs must maintain headquarters within WAEMU region
  - **Source:** [BCEAO Licenses Fintechs](https://www.mfw4a.org/news/bceao-licenses-nine-fintechs-digital-payments-waemu)

- **Licensed Entities in Senegal (as of October 2025):**
  - **Payment Institutions (EDP):** PayDunya, Bictorys
  - **Payment Service Providers:** Mikaty, Flutterwave Senegal
  - **Electronic Money Institution:** InTouch
  - **Regional Status:** 62 institutions authorized across 8 WAEMU countries; Senegal leads with 15 participants
  - **Source:** [PI-SPI Authorized Institutions](https://www.financialafrik.com/en/2025/10/06/pi-spi-list-of-62-institutions-authorized-by-bceao-to-offer-services-to-the-public-in-uemoa/)

- **Application Process:**
  - **Timeline:** Approximately 6 months; approval deemed refused if not granted within 6 months of complete application receipt
  - **Regional Licensing:** WAEMU countries issue licenses independently; fintechs must apply separately in each country for region-wide operations
  - **Source:** [BCEAO Payment License Extension](https://www.ecofinagency.com/news/0206-47086-west-african-central-bank-extends-payment-license-deadline-to-august-31)

- **Microfinance Exception:** Banks, credit institutions, and microfinance groups already under existing financial regulations allowed to continue providing payment services

#### Minister/Governor Statements
- **2019 National Strategy:** Government launched National Strategy for Financial Inclusion targeting increase from 34% to 47% of adult population by 2024 through microfinance and mobile financial services development
- **Source:** [Senegal Banking & Finance 2025](https://practiceguides.chambers.com/practice-guides/banking-regulation-2025/senegal/trends-and-developments)

#### Licensing Requirements
- **Capital:** XOF 10-100 million depending on institution type
- **Cybersecurity:** Stringent compliance obligations mandated
- **Timeline:** 6 months for approval
- **Regional Operations:** Separate application required in each WAEMU country
- **Source:** BCEAO Instruction 001-01-2024

### CÔTE D'IVOIRE (WAEMU/BCEAO)

#### Tax Regime
- **Corporate Tax Rate:** Standard WAEMU corporate tax framework applies
- **VAT:** 18% standard rate (WAEMU harmonized)
- **Banking Sector Tax:** 17% special tax on financial activities (similar to Senegal)
- **Source:** Regional WAEMU tax harmonization framework

#### Central Bank Circulars & Regulations (BCEAO)
- **Regulatory Authority:** BCEAO (same as Senegal - WAEMU regional framework)
- **Minimum Capital Requirements:**
  - **Range:** XOF 10 million to XOF 100 million for payment service providers
  - **Banks:** XOF 20 billion minimum (effective January 1, 2024)
  - **Source:** [BCEAO Electronic Money Institutions](https://www.bceao.int/en/content/electronic-money-issuing-institutions-0)

- **Licensed Entities in Côte d'Ivoire (as of 2025):**
  - **Payment Institutions:** SycaPay, Firstcom Global Payments, Julaya (B2B focused)
  - **Microfinance License:** Djamo (Y Combinator-backed neobank - FIRST fintech to secure microfinance license in WAEMU region)
  - **Regional Leadership:** Côte d'Ivoire hosts 13 of 62 total WAEMU-licensed PI-SPI entities (second after Senegal's 15)
  - **Source:** [Djamo Microfinance License](https://thecondia.com/djamo-first-microfinance-central-bank-bceao/), [BCEAO Licensed Institutions 2024](https://www.bceao.int/sites/default/files/inline-files/List-of-electronic-money-issuing-institutions-as-of-september-30-2024.pdf)

- **Microfinance Approval Process:**
  - **Authority:** Minister approval after document reception
  - **Timeline:** Deemed refused if not pronounced within 6 months from complete file receipt date
  - **Capital Payment:** Share capital must be fully paid up at day of conforming opinion notification
  - **Source:** [WAEMU Microfinance Law Project](https://www.bceao.int/sites/default/files/inline-files/Atelier%20des%2019%20et%2020%20janvier%202023_Projet%20de%20Loi%20microfinance_0.pdf)

- **Licensing Deadline:** September 1, 2025 (only licensed entities allowed to offer payment services)

#### Licensing Requirements
- **Application:** Submit to BCEAO and national authorities
- **Capital:** XOF 10-100 million depending on service type
- **Regional Operations:** Separate license required in each WAEMU country
- **Timeline:** 6 months approval process
- **Source:** BCEAO regional framework

### CAMEROON (CEMAC/COBAC)

#### Tax Regime
- **Corporate Tax Rate:** 33% for companies with turnover above XAF 3 billion
- **Minimum Tax:** 2.2% or 5.5% of turnover (including council surtax) depending on tax regime; prepayment toward final liability (excess not refundable)
- **VAT Rate:** 19.25% total (standard rate + council surtax); exports zero-rated
- **VAT Registration Threshold:** XAF 50 million turnover
- **Withholding Tax on Services (Resident Companies):** 5.5% (5% + 10% council surtax)
- **CEMAC Exception:** NO withholding tax on service fee payments to residents of CEMAC region
- **Distribution Tax on Dividends:** 10% for companies with turnover ≤ XAF 3 billion (reduced from 15%)
- **Money Transfer Tax:** XAF 4 per transaction on transfers by credit/microfinance institutions
- **Source:** [Cameroon Corporate Tax PWC](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/republic-of-cameroon/corporate/taxes-on-corporate-income), [Cameroon Tax Rates](https://www.lloydsbanktrade.com/en/market-potential/cameroon/taxes)

#### Central Bank Circulars & Regulations (COBAC)
- **Regulatory Authority:** Banking Commission of Central Africa (COBAC) for CEMAC zone
- **Key Regulation:** COBAC Regulation No. 01/17/CEMAC/UMAC/COBAC (September 27, 2017) governing microfinance in CEMAC
- **Approval Procedure:** COBAC MFI Regulation R-2017/05 (conditions for approval of MFIs, executives, statutory auditors)

- **MFI Categories:** Three categories with different capital requirements per COBAC Regulation MFI R-2017/03
  - **Note:** Exact capital amounts for each category not publicly detailed in search results; requires direct consultation of R-2017/03
  - **Current Licensed MFIs (December 31, 2024):** 390 licensed MFIs operating in Cameroon across categories 1, 2, and 3
  - **Source:** [Complete Guide MFI Licensing Cameroon 2025](https://dgtcfm.cm/en/microfinance-institution-licensing-cameroon/)

- **Payment Services Capital Requirements:**
  - **Minimum Share Capital:** XAF 500 million (approximately $827,852 USD) to operate as payment service provider
  - **Source:** [Payment Services Regulation Update Cameroon](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=d9c478ed-6761-4988-812a-c78c4813a22d)

- **Licensing Process:**
  - **Application Submission:** To Ministry of Finance (monetary authority) against acknowledgment of receipt
  - **COBAC Review:** Copy transmitted to COBAC for opinion
  - **Approval Flow:** Institutional shuttle (MINFI → COBAC → COBAC → MINFI) within regulatory deadlines
  - **Final Approval:** Issued by Minister of Finance after favorable COBAC opinion
  - **Source:** [Navigating Banking Regulations Cameroon](https://www.hallelaw.com/navigating-banking-regulations-for-startups-in-cameroon-your-complete-guide/)

- **Microfinance-Specific Tax Benefits:**
  - **Collateral/Mortgage Loans (Category 1 MFIs):** Exempted from registration formality and graduated stamp duty
  - **Bad Debt Deductibility:** Threshold increased from XAF 500,000 to XAF 3 million for credit institutions
  - **Provision Deduction:** Prior certification by statutory auditor required when amount ≥ XAF 50 million per year
  - **Source:** [Cameroon Corporate Tax Other Taxes](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/republic-of-cameroon/corporate/other-taxes)

#### Licensing Requirements
- **Application:** Submit to Ministry of Finance with transmission to COBAC
- **Capital:** Per COBAC MFI R-2017/03 (category-specific)
- **Approval:** Minister of Finance after favorable COBAC opinion
- **Timeline:** Per regulatory deadlines (specific duration not detailed in sources)
- **Source:** COBAC regulations 2017

## TIER 3: TANZANIA, UGANDA, RWANDA (EAST AFRICA)

### TANZANIA

#### Tax Regime
- **Corporate Tax Rate:** 30% for resident corporations and PEs
- **Reduced Rates:**
  - **DSE-Listed Companies (30%+ public shares):** 25% for 3 consecutive years
  - **Pharmaceuticals/Leather Manufacturers (with government performance agreement):** 20% for first 5 years
  - **Vehicle/Boat Assembly, Outboard Engines:** 10% for first 5 years
- **Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT):** 1% of turnover for companies with perpetual unrelieved tax losses for current + preceding 2 years

  - **Source:** [Tanzania Double Taxation Agreements](https://www.tra.go.tz/index.php/double-taxation-agreements), [Tanzania Tax Law Guide 2024](https://www.taxriskmanagement.com/tanzania-tax-law/)

  - **Source:** [Draft Microfinance Regulations 2024 Tanzania](https://velmalaw.co.tz/news/draft-microfinance-non-deposit-taking-microfinance-service-providers-regulations-2024/)

## TIER 4: EGYPT, MOROCCO (NORTH AFRICA)

### EGYPT

#### Tax Regime
- **Corporate Tax Rate:** 22.5% flat rate on net taxable income (applies to all businesses including foreign-owned companies, LLCs, branches)
- **VAT Rate:** 14% standard rate
- **Reduced VAT (5%):** Machinery and equipment for manufacturing and service provision (payment suspended on imported or locally purchased items)
- **VAT Registration Threshold:** EGP 500,000 annual turnover
- **Zero-Rated VAT:** Exports of goods/services, goods/services from free zones
- **Withholding Tax:** 20% standard rate
  - **Interest on Loans (Private Sector):** Exempt if >3 years; 20% WHT if <3 years
  - **Royalty Payments:** 20% WHT

- **Stamp Tax:** Two types - nominal (e.g., EGP 1 per contract paper) and proportional (0.05%-0.3% based on transaction value)
- **Bank Loan Stamp Tax:** 0.4% annual proportional stamp tax (shared by bank and client)
- **Source:** [Egypt Tax System GSL](https://gsl.org/en/taxes/egypt/), [Egypt Corporate Tax PWC](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/egypt/corporate/taxes-on-corporate-income)

- **Primary Microfinance Regulator:** FRA under Microfinance Law No. 141 of 2014 (+ amendments)

- **Capital Requirements:**
  - **Non-Banking Fintech Entities (Microfinance under FRA):**
    - **Start-ups (Temporary 2-year license):** EGP 15 million for some activities (less stringent)
    - **Established Market Players:** EGP 75 million for some activities
  - **CBE Payment Service Providers (Category-Based):**
    - **Category A PSPs:** EGP 30 million minimum capital
    - **Category B PSPs:** EGP 10 million minimum capital

  - **Source:** [Egypt Fintech 2024](https://practiceguides.chambers.com/practice-guides/fintech-2024/egypt), [CBE Payment System Rules 2025](https://www.cbe.org.eg/en/news-publications/news/2025/06/19/08/20/psos-and-psps-licensing-rules)

  - **Electronic Payment License:** Required from CBE for fintech entities in micro-lending

  - **Federation Membership:** Must join Egyptian Federation for Financing Medium, Small and Micro Enterprises
  - **Source:** [Microlending Egypt Compliance Guide](https://eg.andersen.com/microlending-in-egypt/)

- **Data Requirements (2024):**
  - **NDPC Guidance (Feb 2024):** Entities processing data for >200 individuals in 6 months OR operating in finance, healthcare, education sectors qualify as Data Controllers and Processors of Major Importance (DCPMIs)
  - **African Union Convention:** Nigeria signed AU Convention on Cybersecurity and Personal Data Protection (Jan 2024) - includes data rights, security, enforcement provisions

- **Application:** Submit to FRA with compliance documentation
- **Capital:** EGP 15 million (start-ups) to EGP 75 million (established players) for fintech microfinance
- **Source:** FRA and CBE regulatory framework

### SOUTH AFRICA

#### Tax Regime
- **Corporate Tax Rate:** 27% flat rate (for tax years ending on/after March 31, 2023; applies to 2024-2025)
- **Small Business Corporations (SBCs):** Tiered structure
  - **0%:** Taxable income up to ZAR 95,750
  - **Incremental rates** up to **27%** on income over ZAR 550,000
- **Special Economic Zones (SEZs):** 15% lowered corporate tax rate
- **VAT Rate:** 15% standard rate (effective from May 1, 2025; proposed increases reversed)
- **VAT Registration Threshold:** ZAR 1,000,000 annual turnover
- **IFRS 9 "Covered Persons" (Banks):** Dividends hedging tax-deductible liabilities taxed to match accounting treatment
- **FLAC Instruments:** Tax treatment clarified for First Loss After Capital instruments mandated by Prudential Authority
- **Global Minimum Tax (Pillar Two - Effective Jan 1, 2024):**
  - **Applicable to:** MNE groups with annual consolidated revenue ≥ EUR 750 million (~ZAR 15 billion)
  - **Top-up Taxes to SARS:** Start 2026/27, projected ZAR 8 billion
  - **Source:** [South Africa Corporate Tax Guide](https://wise.com/gb/blog/south-africa-corporate-tax), [South Africa Corporate Tax PWC](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/south-africa/corporate/taxes-on-corporate-income)

#### Central Bank Circulars & Regulations
- **Regulatory Authorities:**
  - **SARB (Prudential Authority):** Banks, non-bank deposit-taking institutions
  - **National Credit Regulator (NCR):** Micro-lenders, credit providers
  - **MFSA (Microfinance South Africa):** Represents 1800+ microfinance credit provider offices registered with NCR

- **Capital Requirements:**
  - **Banks (SARB-licensed):** ZAR 250 million minimum capital
  - **Mutual Banks:** ZAR 10 million minimum capital
  - **Foreign Bank Subsidiaries/Branches:** ZAR 250 million minimum capital
  - **Minimum Total Capital Requirement:** At least 11.5%
  - **Minimum Leverage Ratio:** At least 4%
  - **Micro-Lenders (NCR-registered):** Less stringent capital requirements (varies by category)
  - **Source:** [Bank Licensing South Africa](https://www.resbank.co.za/content/dam/sarb/what-we-do/prudential-regulation/functions-of-the-prudential-authority/licensing/Banking%20licencing%20in%20the%20Republic%20of%20South%20Africa%20(2021%20update).pdf), [South Africa Lending License](https://blog.lendsqr.com/how-to-get-a-lending-license-in-south-africa/)

- **Microfinance Intermediaries (via SEFA):**
  - **Minimum Operating Period:** 2 years in microfinance lending
  - **Registration:** Registered and operating within South Africa
  - **Compliance:** Companies Act governance requirements, regulatory compliance, risk management policies, board, reviewers
  - **Source:** [SEFA Microfinance Intermediaries](https://www.sefa.org.za/products/micro-finance-intermediaries-mfi)

- **Recent Regulatory Developments (2024):**
  - **March 6, 2024:** PA published Prudential Standard CODI 1 – Fund Liquidity
  - **April 16, 2024:** Directive 3/2024 on minimum requirements for covered deposits and fund liquidity contributions to CODI
  - **FATF Grey-Listing (Early 2023):** South Africa grey-listed; SARB, PA, FIC published guidance to address AML/CFT deficiencies
  - **October 2024 FATF Plenary:** Accepted South Africa's progress report; now deemed largely/fully addressing 16 of 22 action items
  - **Source:** [Banking Laws South Africa 2025](https://www.globallegalinsights.com/practice-areas/banking-and-finance-laws-and-regulations/south-africa/)

#### Licensing Requirements
- **Banks (SARB):** ZAR 250 million capital, stringent governance/risk management compliance
- **Micro-Lenders (NCR):** Mandatory NCR registration; less stringent capital than banks
- **Application Process:** Submit to SARB (banks) or NCR (micro-lenders) with business plan, capital evidence, governance structure
- **Source:** SARB and NCR regulatory framework
